Release Checklist

Writing and Pre-Production

  • Write the song.
  • Finalize lyrics and structure.
  • Create a demo.
  • Confirm songwriting splits with co-writers.
  • Decide who owns the master before recording.

Recording

  • Book studio time or prep your home setup.
  • Track all instruments and vocals.
  • Keep takes organized and clearly labeled.
  • Collect session files from collaborators.
  • Back everything up (local and cloud).

Editing and Mixing

  • Clean up tracks (timing, tuning, noise).
  • Choose reference tracks.
  • Send full sessions or consolidated stems to your mix engineer.
  • Review mix revisions and give clear notes.
  • Approve the final mix and export a high-res WAV.

Mastering

  • Choose a mastering engineer or service.
  • Send the final mix, notes, and any reference tracks.
  • Review master revisions on multiple systems.
  • Approve the final master.
  • Export all versions you need (main, instrumental, clean, TV mix, etc.).

Artwork and Visuals

  • Create or commission cover art that meets distributor specs.
  • Make alternate crops (square, vertical, horizontal) for socials.
  • Prepare basic promo graphics (quote cards, lyric cards, banners).

Metadata and Admin

  • Confirm your artist name and check for conflicts.
  • Finalize the release title and track titles.
  • Confirm all credits (writers, producers, performers, featured artists).
  • Assign ISRCs (or let your distributor assign them).
  • Add a UPC/EAN for the release.
  • Register the song with your PRO.
  • Register with SoundExchange (for U.S. digital performance royalties).

Distribution Setup

  • Choose your distributor.
  • Upload final audio and artwork.
  • Enter all metadata carefully and double-check spelling.
  • Choose release date and time.
  • Set pre-save or pre-order if supported.
  • Submit for store review with enough lead time.

Pitching and Promotion (Pre-Release)

  • Pitch the track through Spotify for Artists at least 7 days before release so it’s eligible for editorial consideration and for automatic placement in your followers’ Release Radar playlists.
  • Update your artist profiles (Spotify, Apple, Amazon, YouTube, etc.).
  • Write or update your artist bio and track description.
  • Build or update your EPK (photos, links, short bio, key stats).
  • Create a promo calendar (teasers, announcement, release-day, follow-ups).
  • Record short-form video content (teasers, snippets, “making of,” live clips).
  • Prepare email newsletter copy for fans.
  • Make a list of blogs, playlists, radio shows, and curators to contact.

Release Week

  • Verify the release is live and correct on all platforms.
  • Update your link-in-bio and website with the new release.
  • Post your release-day announcement.
  • Share behind-the-scenes content and stories about the track.
  • Encourage collaborators to share and tag you.
  • Monitor early stats (streams, saves, playlist adds).
  • Save and share any notable playlist placements or press.

Post-Release

  • Watch for algorithmic placements (Release Radar, Discover Weekly, radio) in Spotify for Artists and other analytics.
  • Add the track to your own playlists and update any “best of” or “essentials” playlists.
  • Send follow-ups to press, blogs, and DJs with early results or quotes.
  • Release alternate versions over time (acoustic, live, remixes) if it fits your plan.
  • Collect fan reactions and screenshots for future promo.

Royalties and Payments

  • Check your distributor’s reporting schedule and payment thresholds.
  • Confirm your payout info (bank, PayPal, etc.) is correct.
  • Set up splits correctly if your distributor supports revenue splits.
  • Track income from:
    • Distributor (master royalties)
    • PRO (performance)
    • Mechanical/publishing admin (if you use one)
    • SoundExchange (digital performance for U.S. recordings)
  • Fix any metadata or ownership issues as soon as they appear.

Long-Term

  • Add the release to your website discography and EPK.
  • Store all final assets (stems, mixes, masters, artwork, lyrics, credits) in long-term backup.
  • Write down what worked and what you’ll change next time.
  • Keep a running live set list and decide where the track fits.
  • Plan how this release connects to the next one (singles, EP, album).
